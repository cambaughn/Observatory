# Remote observation (Street View) — 14102 Delano St, Van Nuys

- **Date:** 2026-07-08 (remote; imagery dated Oct 2025)
- **Method:** Google Street View check of field-program site B-Delano
  (research_methods.md §6, manual form)
- **Observer:** Cam
- **Tracker / finding refs:** RT-11, F21, O2, O3/F16, O8

## Observation

The pin location is the **communal trash-bin staging corner** for the
adjacent apartment building (14102): a long row of green/blue/black bins
lines the curb at the corner (Hazeltine Ave side). In the Oct 2025
imagery the bins are overflowing with boxes/loose items — and **two
dumped couches are visible on the parkway** next to a resident's bin
group a few doors down. The chronic condition predates our 2026 data
window.

## Interpretation (hypotheses, to test on site)

1. **Bin-cluster corners act as dumping anchors ("trash gravity"):** a
   spot already marked as where-discards-go lowers the barrier for
   bulky-item dumping, and items placed beside bins may look
   semi-legitimate ("the truck might take it").
2. Explains the address-stacking: reporters naturally name the building
   whose bins those are (F21) — while the *behavior* belongs to the
   corner, not the parcel.
3. Mechanism candidate for gone-on-arrival (F16): regular collection
   crews may remove staged items before the dumping-ticket crew arrives.
4. Blurs the Item Pickups vs Illegal Dumping boundary (O2): own-building
   disposal beside bins, reported by a neighbor as dumping.
