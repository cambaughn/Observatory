# Weyland Research Questions

This file intentionally contains questions, not tasks.

As questions are answered, new ones should be added.

The project succeeds by improving our understanding of how cities function.

---

## Data Familiarization

- What data is available?
- What does each dataset actually measure?
- What important things are not captured?
- What assumptions or biases exist in the data?

---

## Illegal Dumping

- Where are the worst repeat illegal dumping hotspots?
- Which locations experience the highest recurrence rate?
- Are hotspots stable over time or do they migrate?
- What factors appear to correlate with repeat dumping?

---

## Graffiti

- Which locations receive graffiti repeatedly?
- How quickly is graffiti removed?
- Does removal speed affect recurrence?

---

## Maintenance Performance

- Which request categories take the longest to resolve?
- Which council districts respond fastest?
- Which departments appear to perform best?
- Are there seasonal patterns?

---

## Geographic Questions

- Which neighborhoods generate the most requests?
- Which neighborhoods generate surprisingly few requests?
- Do requests correlate with population, commercial activity, or roadway mileage?
- Where are the largest maintenance deserts?

---

## BID Investigation

- Do BIDs measurably improve cleanliness?
- How do maintenance patterns change across BID boundaries?
- Which BIDs appear most effective?
- What operational practices distinguish them?

---

## Corridor Analysis

- Can we assign an operational health score to an entire corridor?
- Which corridors are improving?
- Which corridors are deteriorating?
- Which corridors experience chronic recurring issues?

---

## Field Investigation

- Which locations should we physically visit?
- Which data patterns contradict reality?
- What observations should become part of our own proprietary dataset?

---

## Long-Term Questions

- Can recurrence be predicted?
- Which interventions appear to have lasting effects?
- Can corridor health be measured objectively?
- What information would cities benefit from collecting that they currently do not?
