## Pattern (hypothesis — not yet a finding)

Chronic sites appear to combine, in varying doses:

1. **Disposal-marked space** — permanent curbside bins/dumpsters ("trash
   gravity", RT-12): the spot is already socially marked for discards.
2. **Semi-private geometry** — walls, medians, alley mouths, recessed
   entrances: unwatched by the street, yet normal to stand at with items.
   (13036 is the type specimen; Sherman Way medians are orphaned space —
   maintenance responsibility ambiguous, nobody's frontage.)
3. **Multifamily frontage** — apartment turnover, shared/overflowing bins,
   no single resident owns the curb.

Deviants: Lankershim (commercial corner + vacancy/decay signal, no bins);
17900 median (pure orphaned space; graffiti on dumped items = layered
disorder).

**Independent validation:** Street View imagery itself captured active
dumping at 4 of 9 sites (Addison, 17808, 17900, Delano) — the 311 counts
correspond to visible physical reality, not just reporting behavior.
Partially answers RT-11 for these sites.

## What would confirm the pattern

- On-site checks (field day): bin permanence, collection days, wall/median
  geometry, sightlines. Prediction to test: dumped items cluster within
  ~10m of bins or in wall/median shadow, not randomly along the block.
- Systematic version (RT-13): score the top ~100 chronic sites citywide on
  a bins / walls-medians-alleys / land-use rubric via Street View, vs a
  random-corner control sample. Cheap, remote, and would turn this
  hypothesis into a finding (or kill it).
